STEP THERAPY DOCUMENTATION
==========================

--- Trial 1 ---
Drug Name:              Triamcinolone acetonide (topical corticosteroid, Class III)
Dose:                   0.1% cream, applied twice daily to affected areas
Start Date:             2022-05-01
End Date:               (ongoing PRN — not primary controller)
Duration of Trial:      Ongoing, 3+ years
Outcome / Reason for Discontinuation:
  Partial / temporary response only. Provides short-term relief during flares but
  no sustained disease control. Prolonged use has caused skin atrophy on face and
  neck. Cannot be used long-term on facial or flexural areas. Inadequate as primary
  maintenance therapy for moderate-to-severe disease extent.

--- Trial 2 ---
Drug Name:              Tacrolimus (Protopic 0.1% ointment — topical calcineurin inhibitor)
Dose:                   0.1% ointment twice daily
Start Date:             2023-03-15
End Date:               2023-09-01
Duration of Trial:      ~5.5 months
Outcome / Reason for Discontinuation:
  Inadequate response. Minimal improvement in EASI score (reduced from 34 to 30
  at 3 months). Patient also reported significant burning/stinging with application
  reducing adherence. Steroid-sparing benefit insufficient for disease severity.

--- Trial 3 ---
Drug Name:              Cyclosporine (Neoral)
Dose:                   3 mg/kg/day (150 mg twice daily)
Start Date:             2024-01-10
End Date:               2024-07-15
Duration of Trial:      6 months
Outcome / Reason for Discontinuation:
  Adverse reaction — nephrotoxicity concern. eGFR declined from 94 to 71 mL/min
  over 5 months. Blood pressure increased to 138/88 mmHg. Drug discontinued per
  nephrologist recommendation. Cyclosporine-induced nephrotoxicity and hypertension
  precludes re-initiation.

--- Trial 4 ---
Drug Name:              Methotrexate
Dose:                   15 mg once weekly (oral), with folic acid 5 mg weekly
Start Date:             2024-09-01
End Date:               2025-01-20
Duration of Trial:      ~4.5 months
Outcome / Reason for Discontinuation:
  Inadequate response and intolerance. EASI score unchanged at 4 months. Patient
  experienced persistent nausea, fatigue, and oral ulcers. Dose escalation not
  attempted due to tolerability. Patient elected to discontinue.
