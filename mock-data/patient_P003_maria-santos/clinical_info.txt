CLINICAL INFORMATION
====================
Symptoms & Severity:
  Patient is a 24-year-old female with lifelong atopic dermatitis, significantly
  worsened over the past 18 months. Widespread eczematous plaques affecting face,
  neck, bilateral antecubital fossae, and trunk. EASI score: 32 (severe). DLQI: 21
  (extremely large effect on quality of life). Reports intense pruritus (8-9/10),
  chronic sleep disruption (averaging 4 hours/night), and significant psychosocial
  impact with social withdrawal. Skin barrier breakdown has led to two secondary
  Staph aureus skin infections in the past year.

Relevant Lab Results:
  - IgE (total): 1,840 IU/mL (markedly elevated; normal <100)  [2026-01-18]
  - Eosinophil count: 0.9 x10^9/L (elevated)                   [2026-01-18]
  - Patch test: Negative for contact allergen sensitization      [2025-11-05]
  - Skin swab (2025-09-10): Staphylococcus aureus — treated with cephalexin x 10 days
  - Skin swab (2026-01-22): Staphylococcus aureus — treated with doxycycline x 7 days

Why Dupilumab (Dupixent) Is Indicated:
  Patient has moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis (EASI 32, DLQI 21) refractory to
  multiple topical and systemic therapies (see Step Therapy). Dupilumab is an IL-4/
  IL-13 receptor antagonist approved for moderate-to-severe AD in patients 6+ years
  inadequately controlled on topical therapies. Strong Th2-driven inflammatory profile
  (elevated IgE, eosinophilia) is consistent with expected responder phenotype.

Why Formulary Alternatives Are Inappropriate:
  - Cyclosporine: Previously trialed — see Step Therapy. Discontinued due to
    nephrotoxicity concern (eGFR decline) and hypertension.
  - Methotrexate: Trialed and failed — see Step Therapy. Inadequate response after
    4 months and patient declined continued use due to side effects.
  - Phototherapy (NB-UVB): Patient unable to attend clinic 3x/week due to full-time
    school and work schedule. No home phototherapy unit accessible.
