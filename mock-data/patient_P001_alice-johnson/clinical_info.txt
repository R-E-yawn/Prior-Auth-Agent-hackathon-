CLINICAL INFORMATION
====================
Symptoms & Severity:
  Patient presents with moderate-to-severe Crohn's disease with persistent abdominal
  cramping (6-8/10 pain scale), 4-6 loose stools per day, rectal bleeding 2-3x/week,
  and significant fatigue. Weight loss of 8 lbs over the past 3 months.

Relevant Lab Results:
  - CRP (C-reactive protein): 42 mg/L (elevated; normal <10)  [2026-02-14]
  - ESR (erythrocyte sedimentation rate): 68 mm/hr (elevated)  [2026-02-14]
  - Hemoglobin: 10.1 g/dL (low; consistent with anemia)        [2026-02-14]
  - Fecal calprotectin: 980 mcg/g (markedly elevated)          [2026-01-30]
  - Colonoscopy (2026-01-15): Active ulceration in ascending and transverse colon,
    moderate-to-severe mucosal inflammation. No stricture or fistula.

Why Adalimumab (Humira) Is Indicated:
  Patient has moderate-to-severe Crohn's disease with objective evidence of active
  inflammation (elevated CRP, calprotectin, endoscopic findings). She has failed
  two lines of conventional therapy (see Step Therapy). Anti-TNF therapy with
  adalimumab is indicated per ACG guidelines for induction and maintenance of
  remission in moderate-to-severe Crohn's disease unresponsive to conventional agents.

Why Formulary Alternatives Are Inappropriate:
  - Infliximab (Remicade): Patient declined IV infusion therapy due to work schedule
    constraints and needle phobia related to IV access. Subcutaneous administration
    preferred for adherence.
  - Vedolizumab: Considered, but slower onset not appropriate given current disease
    activity and nutritional decline. Reserved as second-line biologic.
